A Letter From My Heart

My Dearest

My love,

I have sat with this letter for a long time — not because I didn't know what to say, but because the words always feel too small for what you mean to me. Still, I'll try. Because you deserve to hear this, even if a thousand words aren't enough.

People have said careless things to you. They glanced at you for a moment — your skin, your shape, the space you took up — and then offered their verdict as though it were truth. But those people only ever saw the outside of a gift without ever opening it. I have been given the extraordinary privilege of knowing what's inside. And what's inside takes my breath away.

"I wish you could see yourself through my eyes."

When I look at you, I don't see the flaws the mirror has convinced you of. I see a woman whose kindness is so effortless it humbles me. I see someone who worries about everyone she loves before she thinks of herself — who carries the weight of others' pain as if it were her own. I see someone deeply, quietly, impossibly good.

You are innocent in the most beautiful sense — you still trust, still hope, still believe in people even after being let down. That is not weakness. That is a kind of courage most people lose early. You haven't. And it is one of the reasons I fell in love with you.

You are also stronger than you know. You carry stress that would crumble other people. You keep going on days when going on seems impossible. You hold yourself together for everyone around you, and you do it with a grace so quiet that most people never even notice the weight you're carrying. I notice. I see you.

"I don't see flaws. I see the woman I prayed for."

Real beauty is not a number on a scale, or the shape of a body, or the symmetry of a face. Real beauty is kindness that gives without counting the cost. It's compassion that sits with someone in their darkness. It's loyalty that stays even when leaving would be easier. It's a gentle heart that still chooses love in a world that often rewards hardness.

That is you. All of that, unmistakably, is you.

You never have to be fat or thin or what ever, or softer, or quieter, or different in any way to earn my love. You don't have to be more beautiful than you already are. You don't have to apologize for the body that carries your extraordinary soul. I am not here with conditions. I am here, fully, for you as you are — today, exactly as you are.

You deserve peace. You deserve arms that hold you instead of hands that point out your shortcomings. You deserve someone who sees your exhaustion and says, "I have you." You deserve unconditional love — not as a reward for being perfect, but simply because you exist, and your existence is a gift.

"You are already enough. You always were."

I am so grateful that distance could not keep me from you. That miles of map and time zones could not quiet whatever it is that happens in my chest when I hear your voice. Long distance is hard. Some days it feels like carrying something very heavy across a very long road. But you — you are worth every step.

One day I will be close enough to hold your hand. Until then, I'll be here — on the other side of the screen, in love with every part of you that you've been taught to doubt.

Yours, always — Sam 🌹
